Schedule

The whole event will run from Saturday, February 10, 2018 at 10:00 AM (MST) – Sunday, February 11, 2018 at 3:30 PM (MST)

Here are some important times:

  • ❤Opening Ceremonies — 10:30am – 11am, Saturday, February 10
  • ❤Hacking — 12pm (noon) Saturday, February 10 – 12pm (noon) Sunday, February 11
  • ❤Technical Workshops — 12pm (noon) – 5pm, Saturday, February 10
  • ❤Demos — 12:30pm – 2:30pm, Sunday, February 11
  • ❤Judging — 2:30pm – 3:00pm, Sunday, February 11
  • ❤Closing Ceremonies — 3pm – 3:30pm, Sunday, February 11

You are welcome during the entire event; however, if you wish to maxamize your time, mentors are of the most benefit during the hacking period, a little after hacking begins (~2 hours). This gives the participants some time to settle in, decide on a project, and start working before they run into problems and ask for mentors.

Where

The hackathon will be held in the ATLAS Institute on CU Boulder's campus.

Parking

If you plan on driving, the closest paid parking is Euclid Avenue AutoPark, one block south of the ATLAS building. There is free parking at Lot 308, which is a brief walk to the ATLAS Building.

Deliveries

If you are being dropped off, have deliveries, or cannot walk a long distance from a parking lot, you can stop by the loading dock. The ATLAS Loading Dock is located just south of the ATLAS Building. You can park a vehicle there for up to 15 minutes before moving it to a perment location.

Slack

We'll be using the T9Hacks Slack as our main form of communication at the hackathon. If you've never used Slack before, it's similar to an instant messaging system.
